Partners In Business Conference Reporting Database

Team Name

Kaden Gubler & Claiton Weeks

 

Project Summary

Partners In Business collects information from registrants for each of its 5 annual conferences. Currently this information is stored in a database, which is complicated to access and does not allow for information to be easily searched. In addition to storing the information Partners employees would like to be able to run various reports on the database. Partners In Business also has a unique aspect of its business in what it calls “partnerships” which are essentially prepaid attendance days to any conference, purchased at a reduced rate. These partnerships are currently managed manually by reviewing the attendance of each conference and deducting the days from the partnership. This process is tedious, time consuming and opens the possibility to errors in accounting for them. The business would like to have a more automated way of tracking these partnerships in a database.

 

Project Deliverables

Transaction: Record the registration of an attendee to a Partners In Business conference.

 

Use Case: The attendee enters information on the partners.usu.edu website including name, company, job title, address, phone number, email and partnership number (if applicable), into input fields. This information will be captured into the database when the attendee clicks a submit button.

 

 

Transaction: Record the purchase of partnerships.

 

Use Case: Individuals purchasing a partnership will enter information on the partners.usu.edu website. This information will include name, organization, address, phone number, and email. The user will also select the number of days they would like to purchase a partnership for and whether they would like to purchase now or be sent an invoice. This information will be captured into the database when the user clicks the submit button.

 

Transaction: Running various reports of the information in the database.

 

Use Case: User will enter information into a dashboard such as attendee name, company, and conference. The user will then click a submit button. The information will be used to query the database and return the result of the query on the users screen.
